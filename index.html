The Facts: State Comptroller Audit

In August 2025, the New York State Comptroller released an audit of Pound Ridge's financial practices. Here are the key findings residents need to know:

$745,372
Purchases without competitive bidding
$299,716
Payments not properly reviewed
$54,359
Credit card charges without receipts
8 years
Average 17 months late on financial filings

What the Audit Found

  • Town failed to follow competitive bidding requirements for purchases over $8,000
  • Claims auditing procedures were inadequate, with payments not properly reviewed
  • 11 department heads had Town credit cards with insufficient oversight
  • Financial statements consistently filed late, averaging 17 months behind schedule

What This Means

  • Taxpayer money was spent without proper safeguards
  • Residents lacked timely access to financial information
  • State oversight was necessary due to local failures
  • A Corrective Action Plan is required by November 1, 2025

Our Commitment

The Pound Ridge Party candidates are committed to implementing the State Comptroller's recommendations and ensuring this never happens again. We will:

Implement proper competitive bidding for all purchases over the legal threshold
Establish rigorous claims auditing with board oversight of all payments
File financial statements on time to ensure transparency and compliance
Strengthen internal controls and credit card oversight procedures
