‚ùì What is this audit about?

The NY State Comptroller examined Town purchasing and bill payments from Jan 2022-Nov 2023, reviewing over $10 million in spending and 2,889 payment vouchers to see if proper procedures were followed.

üîç Key Audit Findings

  • Town failed to follow competitive bidding requirements for purchases over $8,000
  • Claims auditing procedures were inadequate, with payments not properly reviewed
  • 11 department heads had Town credit cards with insufficient oversight
  • Financial statements consistently filed late, averaging 17 months behind schedule
  • $745,372 in purchases made without competitive bidding
  • $299,716 in payments not properly reviewed by the board
  • $54,359 in credit card charges without proper receipts or documentation

‚öñÔ∏è Was there fraud or theft?

The audit found no evidence of fraud or misuse of funds. However, the State Comptroller clarified that "these audit results cannot be used to conclude there is no fraud, theft or professional misconduct" because the audit focused on procedures, not fraud detection.

üìù How does the Town respond?

Town Supervisor Hansan emphasized that the audit found "no misuse, misappropriation, or fraudulent use of Town funds" and that all spending was for "legitimate Town purposes." The Town submitted a formal response on July 15, 2025.

üìÖ What happens next?

The Town must create a "Corrective Action Plan" by November 1, 2025, explaining how they will fix these problems. The State Comptroller will monitor implementation of recommended changes.
